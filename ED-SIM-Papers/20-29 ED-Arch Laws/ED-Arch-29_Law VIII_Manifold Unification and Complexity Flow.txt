Overview

Law VIII establishes a global geometric law governing the ED-Arch
phase manifold across all five descriptive layers -- mechanism
classification, drift robustness, temporal structure, sub-mechanism
atlas, and phase boundary geometry -- and across the full parameter
space (N, angle, radius, drift, time).

The central discovery is that these five layers are not independent
structures.  They are redundant projections of a single
N-parameterized geometric object, and N controls a monotone
complexity-reduction flow from folded richness at small N to flat
simplicity at large N.


Formal Statement

Law VIII -- Manifold Unification and the Complexity Flow:

The five ED-Arch descriptive manifolds (mechanism, drift, temporal,
atlas, boundary) describe a single coherent geometric object.  This
object undergoes a monotone complexity-reduction flow parameterized
by N: it is a folded, cusp-rich, drift-fragile surface at N=4, a
resonant plateau at N=8, and a flat, drift-rigid hyperplane at large
N.  The phase boundary, the temporal landscape, the sub-mechanism
taxonomy, the drift sensitivity, and the mechanism classification
are mutually consistent projections of this one surface, with
cross-layer agreement exceeding 98%.


Conceptual Framing

Laws I through VII describe individual features of the ED-Arch
mechanism atlas: the sheet, mirror, and ladder (Law I); the DECAY
resonance (Law II); PBC recurrence windows (Law III); scaling
sensitivity (Law IV); commensurability classes (Law V); the N=4
rotational degeneracy (Law VI); and DECAY exclusivity (Law VII).
Each law identifies a structure within a single layer of the phase
manifold -- a mechanism boundary, a temporal discontinuity, a
sub-mechanism cluster.  Law VIII asks whether these structures are
aspects of one geometric object or artifacts of separate descriptive
frameworks.

The answer is unification.  When all five layers are assembled into a
single tensor indexed by (N, angle, radius, drift) and enriched with
mechanism label, sub-mechanism type, collapse time, boundary distance,
drift sensitivity class, temporal gradient class, and local boundary
curvature, the resulting object exhibits 98.7% cross-layer agreement.
No IC cell appears outside the phase boundary.  No DECAY cell appears
at N > 4.  Every mechanism transition coincides with a sub-mechanism
transition.  The five manifolds do not merely overlap -- they are the
same surface viewed through five different instruments.


Geometric Interpretation

The unified manifold is not a smooth surface at all N.  Its geometry
depends sharply on particle count, and this dependence defines the
complexity flow.

At N=4 the manifold is a folded, multiply-connected object embedded
in the (angle, radius, drift) parameter space.  The IC/non-IC phase
boundary oscillates non-monotonically through radius, producing 3
folds, 8 cusps, and 6 discontinuities in onset angle at drift=0
alone.  Five topological holes punctuate the IC domain -- isolated IC
cells flanked by non-IC at adjacent radii -- confirming that the
boundary is not simply connected.  The sub-mechanism taxonomy is at
its richest: 9 distinct sub-types coexist (IC-bounced at 91% of IC,
OPBC-winding at 61% of OPBC, CR-diagonal at 100% of PBC-corner,
DE-tangent at 82% of DECAY, OL-stalled at 100% of other-late).
The temporal landscape is slow (mean chi = 22.4) and structured, with
a 41 chi-unit cliff at the tangent discontinuity and 239 metastable
cells.  Boundary curvature is extreme (mean kappa = 222, peak = 1000).
Torsion into the drift dimension is large (mean |tau| = 6.8).  The
drift sensitivity is high: a perturbation of d=0.01 changes 25% of
cells, and the tangent discontinuity is destroyed by d=0.02.

At N=8 the manifold flattens abruptly.  The phase boundary becomes a
degenerate surface: onset angle is uniformly +10 degrees at every
radius where IC exists, with zero folds, zero cusps, and zero
discontinuities.  Boundary curvature and torsion both drop to zero.
The sub-mechanism taxonomy contracts from 9 to 8 types.  But the
manifold is not featureless.  It carries a quantitative resonance: a
24% excess of OL-wandering orbits (versus 0-1.4% at all other N),
producing a slow temporal plateau (mean chi = 2.74 at positive
angles, 8x slower than N=12) that persists unchanged across all
tested drifts.  This resonance is not a new mechanism -- it is a
quantitative anomaly in the population of an existing mechanism,
visible simultaneously in all five layers.  The IC domain is patchy,
with radius gaps at 0.10, 0.15, 0.30, 0.90, 0.95 that produce the
highest multi-valued count in the dataset (45 slices).

At N=20 the manifold converges to a trivial hyperplane.  The phase
boundary is flat and drift-invariant.  The taxonomy collapses to 5
sub-types: IC-direct alone accounts for 100% of IC (no bounced
orbits survive), OPBC-single accounts for 91% of OPBC, and
OL-wandering vanishes entirely.  Mean chi drops to 0.20 -- a
112-fold compression from N=4.  Curvature, torsion, folds, cusps,
and temporal cliffs are all identically zero.  Drift rigidity reaches
82%.  The manifold has shed every non-trivial geometric feature.


Empirical Grounding

The unification test was conducted across 1,980 parameter cells
(4 values of N, 9 angles, 11 radii, 5 drifts), each carrying data
from both the sweep files (mechanism, chi, dR) and the atlas files
(PBC crossings, corner sequences, winding numbers, R-extrema,
periodicity scores, multi-stage flags, tangent crossings).  For
every cell, seven derived quantities were computed: mechanism label,
sub-mechanism type, collapse time, signed boundary distance, drift
sensitivity class, temporal gradient class, and local boundary
curvature.

Cross-layer agreement was tested by four independent criteria.
First, mechanism-to-temporal-class determinism: the modal temporal
class captures 41-62% of cells per mechanism, indicating that
mechanism constrains but does not fully determine temporal behavior.
Second, subtype-to-chi-range overlap: 17 of 45 subtype pairs share
overlapping chi ranges, confirming that sub-mechanisms partition
parameter space more finely than collapse time alone.  Third,
boundary-distance sign consistency: IC cells appear only at
non-positive boundary distance (0 of 65 exterior cells are IC across
all N), confirming the phase boundary is a true separatrix.  Fourth,
overall logical consistency: 98.7% of cells satisfy all cross-layer
constraints simultaneously (94.9% at N=4, 100% at N>=8).

The 5.1% inconsistency at N=4 is concentrated at the tangent
discontinuity, where DECAY cells at chi=100 abut fast IC cells at
chi<1 -- a boundary so sharp that the discrete angle grid (1-degree
resolution) cannot fully resolve it.  This is a resolution artifact,
not a structural contradiction.


Cross-N Structure

The complexity flow follows a strict hierarchy:

  N=4:  folded, 3 folds, 8 cusps, 9 sub-types, chi=22.4,
        60% drift-rigid, 5 boundary holes, kappa=222

  N=8:  flat boundary, 0 folds, 0 cusps, 8 sub-types, chi=2.74,
        55% drift-rigid, patchy IC domain, OL-wandering resonance

  N=12: flat, 0 folds, 0 cusps, 8 sub-types, chi=1.05,
        69% drift-rigid, contiguous IC domain

  N=20: flat, 0 folds, 0 cusps, 5 sub-types, chi=0.20,
        82% drift-rigid, trivial hyperplane

Every measured complexity metric -- number of sub-types, number of
folds, boundary curvature, torsion, mean chi, metastable cell count,
multi-valued slice count -- decreases monotonically from N=4 to N=20.
The transition is sharp: the manifold loses all folds, cusps, and
high curvature between N=4 and N=8.  After N=8, simplification is
gradual.

The IC subtype crossover tracks this flow in lockstep.  At N=4,
IC-bounced dominates (91% of IC) because the folded boundary
produces PBC crossings before collapse.  At N=8, IC-direct rises to
43%.  At N=12, IC-direct overtakes IC-bounced (77%).  At N=20,
IC-direct reaches 100% -- all collapses are single-step direct
contractions, and no particle ever crosses a PBC boundary before
merging.


Drift Clause

Drift reveals the fragility gradient of the unified manifold.

At N=4, the manifold is drift-fragile.  A perturbation of d=0.01
changes 25% of cells, collapses the tangent discontinuity from a
near-universal sheet to a weak remnant, and shifts DECAY from
angle=0 to angle=-1.  By d=0.02, the tangent discontinuity is
destroyed and DECAY vanishes from most radii.  The mean IC onset
angle shifts by -3.0 degrees at d=0.10, and the only negative-angle
IC onset in the entire dataset appears at r=0.10.  Drift does not
create new topological complexity; it erodes existing complexity by
smoothing folds and filling boundary gaps.

At N>=8, the manifold is drift-rigid.  Mean onset shift is 0.0 at
all tested drifts.  The N=8 OL-wandering plateau remains at 39-49%
across all drift values.  At N=20, drift invariance is absolute:
not a single onset angle changes across the five tested drift levels.
Drift neither creates nor destroys features at large N because there
are no features left to perturb.


Temporal Clause

The temporal manifold tracks the complexity flow but carries finer
structure than the mechanism boundary alone.

Mean collapse time compresses 112-fold from N=4 (chi=22.4) to N=20
(chi=0.20), with IC-specific scaling steeper than 1/N^2.  Temporal
gradients are universally CLIFF-class at N=4 (maximum gradient ~92
chi-units across a single angle step), MODERATE at N=8 (~9), and
GENTLE at N=20 (~1.3).  Metastable regions (chi>5 near mechanism
boundaries) are abundant at N=4 (239 cells), modest at N=8 (41
cells), and absent at N>=20.

The one discordance between the temporal layer and the boundary layer
is that the phase boundary is flat at N>=8 while the temporal
manifold retains folds: chi varies non-monotonically across radius
even where the onset angle is constant at +10 degrees.  This means
the temporal landscape preserves geometric information that the
binary mechanism label has discarded.  The temporal layer is the
highest-resolution projection of the unified manifold.

The chi ratio across the IC/non-IC phase boundary is 0.8-0.9x at
all N, confirming that the phase boundary is not a temporal cliff.
Collapse time varies continuously across the mechanism boundary.
The large temporal discontinuities identified in earlier analyses
(the +41 chi-unit jump at N=4, angle=0) are internal to the IC
domain, not at the IC/non-IC interface.


Exceptions and Degeneracies

DECAY is the sole structural exception to the unified manifold's
smooth complexity flow.  It occupies a 1D filament at (N=4, angle=0,
all radii, drift=0) and is confined exclusively to N=4 by Law VII.
Within the unified tensor, DECAY cells have chi=100.0 (the timeout
ceiling), DE-tangent subtype (82%), extreme boundary curvature
(kappa=287), and zero drift tolerance (destroyed by d=0.02).  DECAY
is topologically isolated from the IC domain at all N except N=4,
where it shares boundary through the tangent discontinuity.  It is
the only feature of the unified manifold that is not smoothly
connected to its neighbors.

The N=8 resonance is not an exception but a plateau: a quantitative
excess of OL-wandering orbits (24% versus 0-1.4% at other N) that
produces a slow temporal band and a patchy IC domain.  Unlike DECAY,
the N=8 anomaly does not introduce a new mechanism or break any
cross-layer consistency.  It is fully embedded in the unified
surface, visible as a broad hump rather than a singularity.

The boundary is multi-valued at all N: at a given angle and drift,
IC and non-IC coexist at different radii.  This multi-valuedness is
a structural invariant of the manifold, not a pathology.  It means
the phase boundary cannot be expressed as a single-valued function
onset(radius) at any N.


Closing Synthesis

Law VIII completes the geometric description of ED-Arch by
establishing that its five descriptive frameworks -- mechanism
classification, phase boundary continuation, temporal landscape,
sub-mechanism atlas, and drift robustness -- are not independent
theories.  They are five projections of one geometric object.

This object is an N-parameterized surface in (angle, radius, drift)
space whose complexity decreases monotonically with particle count.
At N=4 it is a folded, cusp-rich manifold with 9 sub-mechanism
families, slow dynamics, drift-fragile boundaries, and the singular
DECAY filament.  At N=8 it carries a quantitative resonance --
visible in all five layers simultaneously -- that produces a slow
plateau without introducing new mechanisms.  At large N it converges
to a trivial hyperplane where a single sub-mechanism (IC-direct)
dominates, collapse is instantaneous (chi ~ 0.01), boundaries are
flat and drift-invariant, and the taxonomy has collapsed from 9
families to 5.

The complexity flow is the central organizing principle.  Every
structure identified in Laws I through VII -- the sheet, the ladder,
the DECAY islands, the scaling sensitivity, the commensurability
classes, the N=4 degeneracy, the DECAY exclusivity -- is a feature
of this one surface at a specific value of N.  Law VIII unifies them
into a single geometric statement:

The ED-Arch phase manifold is one surface, and N is the dial
that controls its complexity.
