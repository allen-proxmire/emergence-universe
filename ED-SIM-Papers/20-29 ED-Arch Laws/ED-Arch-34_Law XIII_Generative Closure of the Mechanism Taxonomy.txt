Overview

Laws IX through XII establish the three-coordinate generative hierarchy
of the ED-Arch manifold: lambda for temporal regime, angle for mechanism
sector, and PBC geometry for fine structure and boundary modulation.
Law XII showed that every sub-mechanism type traces to one of these
three coordinates and that no feature in the 45,031-cell dataset resists
explanation.  Law XIII draws the structural consequence of this
completeness.

The ED-Arch mechanism taxonomy is closed.  Exactly 10 sub-mechanism
types exist across the full parameter space, and each is the necessary
geometric outcome of a specific combination of approach direction, PBC
interaction mode, and trajectory regime.  No 11th type can appear
because every possible combination of these structural ingredients has
already been realized.  The 10 types are not an empirical catalogue
waiting for the next survey to discover an exception.  They are the
complete set of structurally distinct collapse geometries that a ring
of particles on a periodic box can produce.

Law XIII is the closure theorem of the ED-Arch formalism.  It asserts
that the three-coordinate hierarchy is not merely sufficient to explain
the observed taxonomy but is exhaustive: the coordinates generate
exactly the 10 types, no more and no fewer, and the taxonomy cannot
grow under any extension of the parameter space to new N values, new
radii, new angles, or new drifts.


Formal Statement

Law XIII -- Generative Closure of the Mechanism Taxonomy:

The ED-Arch manifold admits exactly 10 sub-mechanism types, generated
by the three-coordinate hierarchy (lambda, angle, PBC geometry), and
no additional types can arise under any combination of N, lambda,
angle, radius, or drift.  Three types are angle-protected cores set by
the velocity decomposition.  Three are PBC-proximity corrections set
by edge and corner interactions.  Three are lambda >> 1 trajectory
types that require extended paths through the periodic lattice.  One
is a lambda ~ 1 resonance type sustained by the boundary band's
near-tangential launch directions.  The taxonomy is structurally
finite because the number of approach directions is finite (three),
the number of PBC interaction modes is finite (two), and the number
of trajectory regimes is finite (three), and all products of these
finite sets have been enumerated.


Conceptual Framing

The question that Law XIII answers is not "how many sub-mechanism
types have we observed?" but "how many can exist?"  The distinction
matters.  An empirical count of observed types is always provisional:
the next sweep might discover a new one.  A structural closure argument
is definitive: it identifies the generative ingredients, enumerates
their combinations, and shows that every combination has already been
realized.

The generative ingredients of the ED-Arch taxonomy are three.

The first ingredient is the approach direction.  At the moment of
collapse, two particles approach each other along a displacement
vector that has three structurally distinct orientations relative to
the ring center: inward (toward the center, producing IC mechanisms),
outward (away from the center, producing OPBC mechanisms), and
tangential (along the ring, producing OL mechanisms).  These three
directions exhaust the possibilities because the displacement vector
in two dimensions has only two components -- radial and tangential --
and the radial component has only three qualitative states: positive,
negative, and zero.  No fourth approach direction exists.

The second ingredient is the PBC interaction mode.  During collapse,
the approaching pair may interact with PBC features in three
structurally distinct ways: no interaction (the pair merges within the
interior of the PBC cell), edge interaction (the pair merges across a
single PBC wall), or corner interaction (the pair merges through a PBC
corner where two walls meet).  These three interaction modes exhaust
the PBC topology of a rectangular box in two dimensions.  A 2D
rectangular PBC cell has exactly two types of codimension-1 features
(horizontal and vertical edges, which are equivalent by the square
symmetry of the box) and one type of codimension-2 feature (the four
corners, equivalent by symmetry).  Interior, edge, and corner are the
complete list.

The third ingredient is the trajectory regime.  The lambda invariant
determines whether the collapse trajectory is instantaneous
(lambda << 1), marginal (lambda ~ 1), or extended (lambda >> 1).
At lambda << 1, the trajectory is a single step and the mechanism is
determined by the initial configuration alone.  At lambda ~ 1, the
trajectory is long enough for boundary-band orbits to wander before
collapsing.  At lambda >> 1, the trajectory is long enough to wind
through multiple PBC cells, producing complex multi-bounce and
multi-crossing pathways.  These three regimes exhaust the structural
possibilities because lambda is a monotone function of N that
traverses each regime exactly once.

The 10 sub-mechanism types are the realized products of these three
ingredients.  Not every product is structurally distinct -- some
combinations collapse to the same observable type -- but every
structurally distinct combination produces exactly one of the 10
observed types.  The taxonomy is closed because the ingredient sets
are finite and their product is fully enumerated.


Geometric Interpretation

The 10 sub-mechanism types can be organized into a generative table
indexed by approach direction, PBC interaction, and trajectory regime.

At lambda << 1 (instantaneous collapse, proximity-PBC):

Inward approach with no PBC interaction produces IC-direct.  The ring
contracts and the nearest pair merges through the interior of the PBC
cell.  This is the minimal inward pathway -- the mechanism that would
occur on an infinite plane.  It accounts for 56.2% of all N >= 48
cells and is the dominant mechanism at all positive angles.

Inward approach with edge interaction produces IC-bounced.  The ring
contracts but the collapsing pair straddles a PBC wall, so the merger
crosses the wall.  The trajectory is still inward in net direction but
involves one PBC crossing.  IC-bounced accounts for 7.7% of all
N >= 48 cells and occurs exclusively at radii where edge proximity is
active.

Outward approach with edge interaction produces OPBC-single.  The ring
expands and the nearest pair merges across the nearest PBC wall.  This
is the minimal outward pathway.  It accounts for 22.6% of all N >= 48
cells and is the dominant mechanism at all negative angles.

Outward approach with near-corner interaction produces OPBC-glancing.
The ring expands and the outward trajectory passes near a PBC corner,
producing a deflected but not fully diagonal merger.  OPBC-glancing
accounts for 2.1% of all N >= 48 cells and is confined to intermediate
radii and negative angles.

Any approach with corner interaction produces CR-diagonal.  The
collapsing pair is nearest to a PBC corner, and the merger occurs
along the diagonal direction.  CR-diagonal is the only mechanism
whose approach direction is not determined by the sign of v_r; it is
determined by corner proximity overriding the radial velocity.  It
accounts for 4.7% of all N >= 48 cells and appears at both positive
and negative angles.

Tangential approach with any PBC mode produces OL-stalled.  The radial
velocity is near zero and the pair merges through whichever PBC feature
is nearest -- but because the approach is tangential, the mechanism is
classified as OL regardless of the PBC interaction.  OL-stalled
accounts for 6.7% of all N >= 48 cells and is concentrated at
gamma = 0.

These six types are the lambda << 1 attractor: the irreducible set
of collapse geometries that survive when trajectories are too short
to develop any path-dependent structure.

At lambda ~ 1 (marginal collapse, resonance-PBC):

Tangential approach with extended PBC interaction produces
OL-wandering.  Near-tangential trajectories at the lambda ~ 1
timescale execute multiple orbits, interacting repeatedly with PBC
walls, before eventually collapsing.  OL-wandering is structurally
distinct from OL-stalled: the collapse occurs after an extended
wandering phase rather than immediately.  It accounts for 21% of all
N = 8 cells and vanishes by N = 12 (dropping to 1.4%) as lambda falls
below the marginal threshold.  OL-wandering is the only mechanism
that requires both the boundary band (for near-tangential launch) and
the lambda ~ 1 regime (for sufficient trajectory lifetime).

At lambda >> 1 (extended collapse, trajectory-PBC):

Outward approach with multiple PBC crossings produces OPBC-winding.
The expanding ring sends particles through multiple PBC cells, winding
around the periodic domain before merging.  OPBC-winding requires
trajectories long enough to traverse multiple PBC cell widths, which
requires lambda >> 1.  At lambda << 1, the same outward approach
merges at the nearest single wall (OPBC-single) because the trajectory
is too short to reach the second wall.

Tangential approach with zero drift at lambda >> 1 produces DE-tangent.
The particle is launched exactly tangent to the ring with zero radial
component and zero drift.  At lambda >> 1, this produces a trajectory
that orbits the ring without contracting or expanding, reaching the
DECAY timeout (chi = 100) without merging.  DE-tangent is the
canonical DECAY variant: the perfectly balanced tangential orbit that
neither collapses nor escapes.

Tangential approach with near-zero velocity at lambda >> 1 produces
DE-frozen.  This is DECAY at angle = 0, drift = 0, and specific radii
where the initial configuration places the ring at a metastable
equilibrium.  DE-frozen differs from DE-tangent in that the particles
barely move at all rather than orbiting; they are effectively frozen
in place until the timeout.

These three types are the lambda >> 1 exclusives: mechanisms that
require extended trajectories through the PBC lattice and are
eliminated by the lambda = 1 crossover.  DE-tangent and DE-frozen are
confined to N = 4 by Law VII.  OPBC-winding persists faintly at N = 8
but vanishes by N = 12.


Lambda-Driven Mechanisms Clause

The four lambda-sensitive mechanisms (OL-wandering, OPBC-winding,
DE-tangent, DE-frozen) share a common structural requirement:
trajectories long enough to develop path-dependent structure.

At lambda >> 1, the mean inter-particle spacing exceeds the merge
threshold by a factor of approximately 5 (at N = 4) to 1.3 (at N = 8).
Particles launched from the ring must traverse this distance before
encountering a neighbor, and the trajectory during this transit is
shaped by every PBC wall, corner, and image it encounters.  The
trajectory is the mechanism: IC-bounced at lambda >> 1 involves
multi-step PBC crossings that differ qualitatively from the
single-step crossing at lambda << 1.  OPBC-winding involves multiple
cell traversals that are impossible in a single timestep.  DE-tangent
involves a sustained orbit that requires hundreds of timesteps to
timeout.

The lambda = 1 crossover eliminates these trajectory-dependent types
in a single step.  At N = 8 (lambda ~ 1.3), OL-wandering persists
at 21% but OPBC-winding is already rare.  At N = 12 (lambda ~ 0.58),
OL-wandering drops to 1.4% and OPBC-winding vanishes.  At N = 20
(lambda ~ 0.21), all four lambda-sensitive types are gone except for
trace OL-wandering at 0.2%.  By N = 48 (lambda ~ 0.04), the taxonomy
has stabilized at the 6 asymptotic survivors and remains there through
N = 128.

The elimination is sharp, not gradual.  Taxonomy diversity drops from
9 types at N = 4 to 8 at N = 8 (loss of DE-tangent and DE-frozen,
which are N = 4 exclusive) to 6 at N >= 20 (loss of OL-wandering and
OPBC-winding).  The faint N = 24 echo of OL-wandering (3.2%) is the
last trace of the lambda ~ 1 resonance before the taxonomy locks to
its asymptotic composition.  The 10-to-6 contraction is not a gradual
attrition of rare types; it is the structural consequence of the
trajectory length dropping below the thresholds required for winding,
wandering, and tangential stalling.


Angle-Driven Mechanisms Clause

The three angle-protected core mechanisms (IC-direct, OPBC-single,
OL-stalled) are structural necessities of the velocity decomposition
and cannot be eliminated by any parameter change.

IC-direct exists because the velocity decomposition has a positive
radial component at positive angles.  As long as particles are
launched inward (gamma > 0), contraction occurs, and if no PBC wall
intervenes, the merger is direct.  IC-direct is the default inward
mechanism: it requires no special PBC configuration and no special
trajectory structure.  It persists at every N, every radius where edge
and corner proximity are absent, and every positive angle.  At the
maximally symmetric radius r = 0.500, where PBC effects vanish,
IC-direct accounts for 100% of the inward sector.

OPBC-single exists because the velocity decomposition has a negative
radial component at negative angles.  As long as particles are
launched outward (gamma < 0), expansion occurs, and the nearest PBC
wall provides the merger partner.  OPBC-single is the default outward
mechanism: it requires only one PBC wall crossing and no corner
interaction.  It persists at every N, every radius, and every negative
angle.

OL-stalled exists because the velocity decomposition has a zero
radial component at gamma = 0.  Near-tangential launch produces no net
inward or outward motion, and collapse occurs through whatever PBC
feature is nearest -- but the approach geometry is tangential, not
radial, producing a mechanism label that is structurally distinct from
both IC and OPBC.  OL-stalled is the default boundary-layer mechanism:
it persists at gamma = 0 at every N and every radius.

These three cores are topologically protected.  They correspond to
the three sign states of the radial velocity component (positive,
negative, zero), which are the three qualitative states of a
one-dimensional continuous function.  No continuous deformation of the
manifold can eliminate any of them without eliminating the
corresponding velocity sector.  The angle partition that produces
them is a property of the initial conditions, not of the dynamics,
and it survives every value of lambda.


PBC-Driven Mechanisms Clause

The three PBC-proximity correction mechanisms (IC-bounced,
OPBC-glancing, CR-diagonal) are structural consequences of the
periodic box's edge-and-corner geometry and cannot be eliminated by
any increase in N or any change in angle.

IC-bounced exists because the PBC box has edges.  When the circumradius
places a particle near a box edge, the nearest PBC image lies across
that edge, and the inward contraction crosses the wall before merging.
IC-bounced is IC-direct modulated by a single edge crossing.  It
cannot be eliminated by increasing N because the edge-proximity
condition depends on the circumradius-to-box ratio, not on the
inter-particle spacing.  As N increases at fixed radius, the
circumradius R = d_px / (2 * sin(pi/N)) changes, and different
particles cycle through the edge-proximity zone.  IC-bounced
oscillates in frequency but never vanishes: at every N >= 12, at least
one tested radius places particles near an edge.

OPBC-glancing exists because the outward sector's PBC interactions
include near-corner geometries.  When a particle expanding outward
passes near but not through a box corner, the glancing PBC interaction
deflects the trajectory without producing a full diagonal collapse.
OPBC-glancing is OPBC-single modulated by a partial corner
interaction.  It is confined to intermediate radii and negative angles,
and accounts for 2.1% of all N >= 48 cells.

CR-diagonal exists because the PBC box has corners.  When the
circumradius places a particle near a box corner, the diagonal PBC
image is closer than any ring neighbor, and collapse occurs along the
diagonal.  CR-diagonal is the only mechanism whose approach direction
is set by PBC geometry rather than by the radial velocity.  It is
topologically distinct from IC and OPBC because the displacement vector
is diagonal (approximately 45 degrees) rather than radial or
tangential.  CR-diagonal accounts for 4.7% of all N >= 48 cells and
appears at both positive and negative angles.

The three PBC corrections are permanent because the PBC box is
permanent.  A 2D rectangular periodic cell has exactly two types of
geometric features that a collapsing pair can interact with: edges
(codimension-1, four equivalent edges) and corners (codimension-2,
four equivalent corners).  Each feature produces a distinct correction
to each approach direction: edges produce IC-bounced (inward + edge),
OPBC-single (outward + edge, but this is the core mechanism itself),
and OPBC-glancing (outward + near-corner); corners produce CR-diagonal
(any direction + corner).  The PBC topology generates exactly three
correction types beyond the three cores.  No additional PBC feature
exists in two dimensions -- there are no codimension-3 features in a
2D box -- so no additional correction type can arise.


Taxonomy-Closure Clause

The mechanism taxonomy is closed at exactly 10 types.  This closure is
structural, not empirical.

The structural argument proceeds in three steps.

Step 1: The approach direction has exactly three qualitative states.
The radial component of the collapse displacement vector is a
continuous scalar that can be positive (inward), negative (outward),
or zero (tangential).  These three states partition the mechanism space
into three sectors.  No fourth state exists because the radial
component is a one-dimensional quantity.

Step 2: The PBC interaction has exactly three qualitative states.
The collapsing pair's interaction with the PBC box is one of:
interior (no PBC feature involved), edge (one PBC wall crossed), or
corner (two PBC walls meeting at a point).  These three states
exhaust the topology of a 2D rectangular PBC cell.  The interior is
the open cell.  The edges are the four codimension-1 faces (pairwise
equivalent).  The corners are the four codimension-2 vertices
(pairwise equivalent).  There are no codimension-3 features in two
dimensions.

Step 3: The trajectory regime has exactly three qualitative states.
Lambda determines whether the collapse is instantaneous (lambda << 1),
marginal (lambda ~ 1), or extended (lambda >> 1).  These three regimes
are traversed monotonically as N increases, with no re-entrant
transitions.  Each regime selects a subset of the 3 x 3 direction-PBC
product: instantaneous collapse cannot produce multi-crossing or
winding pathways, and marginal collapse can produce only the wandering
variant.

The full product 3 (directions) x 3 (PBC modes) x 3 (regimes) = 27
formal combinations.  Most of these are either structurally
indistinguishable (e.g., inward + interior at all three regimes
produces IC-direct at all three, because the PBC interaction is absent
regardless of trajectory length) or structurally impossible (e.g.,
tangential + corner + instantaneous, which would require a tangential
launch to reach a distant corner in a single timestep).  The 27
formal combinations reduce to exactly 10 structurally distinct and
physically realizable types.

The reduction proceeds as follows.  Of the 9 direction-PBC pairs at
lambda << 1: inward-interior is IC-direct, inward-edge is IC-bounced,
inward-corner merges with CR-diagonal (corner proximity overrides the
inward direction), outward-interior does not occur (outward expansion
always reaches a PBC wall), outward-edge is OPBC-single, outward-corner
has two variants (CR-diagonal when the corner image is closest,
OPBC-glancing when the edge image is closer but the corner deflects),
tangential-interior is OL-stalled, tangential-edge is OL-stalled (the
tangential classification overrides the PBC label), and
tangential-corner is OL-stalled (same reasoning).  This produces 6
distinct types at lambda << 1.

At lambda ~ 1, the marginal trajectory regime adds one new type:
OL-wandering, which is tangential approach with extended PBC
interaction at marginal timescale.  OL-wandering is distinct from
OL-stalled because the trajectory involves multiple PBC interactions
before collapse, producing observable wandering rather than immediate
stalling.

At lambda >> 1, the extended trajectory regime adds three new types:
OPBC-winding (outward + multiple PBC crossings), DE-tangent (tangential
+ sustained PBC orbit), and DE-frozen (tangential + metastable PBC
equilibrium).  OPBC-winding is distinct from OPBC-single because the
trajectory winds through multiple PBC cells.  DE-tangent and DE-frozen
are distinct from OL-stalled because the trajectory is sustained for
the full DECAY timeout rather than collapsing immediately.

Total: 6 (lambda << 1) + 1 (lambda ~ 1) + 3 (lambda >> 1) = 10.

This enumeration is exhaustive.  Every combination of approach
direction, PBC interaction, and trajectory regime has been assigned
to one of the 10 types or shown to be structurally indistinguishable
from one of them.  No combination remains unassigned.

The empirical evidence confirms the closure.  Across 45,031 cells
spanning 14 N values (4-128), 21 angles (-10 to +15 degrees), 17
radii (0.025 to 0.950), and 8 drift values (0 to 0.030), exactly 10
sub-mechanism types appear.  The Law IX consolidation sweep added
11,970 cells at non-canonical parameters; the Law X consolidation
sweep added 11,102 cells at boundary-layer and beyond-grid parameters.
No new type appeared in any extension.  The taxonomy is closed.


Temporal Clause

The taxonomy's temporal structure is entirely governed by lambda, as
established in Law IX.  Law XIII adds the observation that the temporal
structure is consistent with the closure argument: each lambda regime
selects a specific subset of the 10 types, and the subsets are nested.

At lambda << 1 (N >= 48): the taxonomy is {IC-direct, IC-bounced,
OPBC-single, OPBC-glancing, CR-diagonal, OL-stalled}.  Chi is at the
measurement floor (0.01) for 99.9% of cells.  All 6 types collapse
within a single timestep.  The temporal layer carries no information
about mechanism identity.

At lambda ~ 1 (N = 8): the taxonomy is the 6 asymptotic types plus
OL-wandering, for a total of 7 active types.  Mean chi is 2.74 at
positive angles, reflecting the extended trajectories that produce
OL-wandering.  The temporal layer carries the resonance signature.

At lambda >> 1 (N = 4): the taxonomy is all 10 types.  Mean chi
ranges from 0.01 to 100 (the DECAY timeout), reflecting the full
range of trajectory lengths.  The temporal layer is maximally
informative: chi varies with angle, radius, and drift, and the
temporal gradient encodes the mechanism partition at high resolution.

The nesting lambda << 1 subset lambda ~ 1 subset lambda >> 1 is
strict.  No type that appears at lambda << 1 is absent at lambda >> 1.
No type that appears only at lambda >> 1 appears at lambda << 1.
The complexity-reduction flow of Law VIII, expressed in taxonomic terms,
is the monotone elimination of lambda-sensitive types as the system
crosses the lambda = 1 threshold, leaving only the proximity-PBC
and angle-protected types as permanent survivors.

The 6 asymptotic survivors are the lambda << 1 attractor of the
taxonomy.  They are the mechanisms that require no trajectory structure
-- only the initial configuration of particles relative to the PBC
box.  The 4 lambda-sensitive types are the transient excess of the
lambda > 1 regime, eliminated exactly at the lambda = 1 crossover.
The attractor is unique: there is no alternative set of 6 or fewer
types that the taxonomy could converge to, because the 3 cores are
topologically protected and the 3 proximity corrections are PBC-
topology protected.


Exceptions and Degeneracies

The closure argument assumes a 2D rectangular PBC box.  On a hexagonal
PBC domain, the corner topology would differ (three edges meeting at
120-degree vertices rather than two at 90 degrees), and the
CR-diagonal mechanism would be replaced by a hexagonal-corner
mechanism.  The total count of sub-types would likely differ.  Law XIII
is specific to the square PBC geometry of the ED-Arch simulation.

The closure argument assumes ballistic evolution (x += v * dt) with
no inter-particle forces.  If particles experienced attractive or
repulsive forces, the approach geometry at collapse would depend on the
force law, not just the initial velocity, and additional mechanism
types could arise (e.g., spiral infall for attractive forces, or
oscillatory stalling for repulsive forces).  Law XIII is specific to
the force-free ballistic dynamics of the ED-Arch engine.

The 10-type count includes DE-tangent and DE-frozen as separate types.
These two are closely related -- both are DECAY variants at gamma = 0,
drift = 0, N = 4 -- and could reasonably be merged into a single
DECAY type, reducing the count to 9.  The distinction is maintained
because the geometric signatures differ (orbiting vs. frozen) and the
radius dependence differs (DE-tangent at most radii, DE-frozen at
specific metastable radii).  Whether the count is 9 or 10 depends on
the granularity of the classification scheme; the closure argument
holds at either resolution.

The maximally symmetric radius r = 0.500 reduces the active taxonomy
to 3 types at any N >= 48, demonstrating that the PBC-correction types
are not structurally necessary at every parameter point -- they are
necessary only in the aggregate over all radii.  The closure claim is
that 10 types exist across the full parameter space, not that 10 types
coexist at every parameter point.

N = 4 produces 9 of the 10 types (all except OL-wandering, which
requires lambda ~ 1).  N = 8 produces 8 of the 10 types (the 7 active
types plus traces of OPBC-winding).  No single N value produces all
10 types simultaneously.  The 10-type taxonomy is realized only across
the union of all N values, reflecting the fact that different lambda
regimes activate different subsets.

The non-canonical parameters tested in the consolidation sweeps
(radii 0.025 to 0.950, angles -10 to +15 degrees, drifts 0 to 0.030)
span a range broad enough to activate all PBC-proximity effects and
all boundary-band phenomena.  However, the closure argument is
structural, not statistical: it does not depend on having sampled
"enough" parameter points.  It depends on having enumerated all
structurally distinct approach-direction x PBC-interaction x
trajectory-regime combinations, which is a finite combinatorial
exercise.


Closing Synthesis

Law XIII is the terminal law of the ED-Arch formalism.  It closes the
system by showing that the three-coordinate generative hierarchy
produces a finite, enumerable, and complete mechanism taxonomy.

The ED-Arch manifold is generated by three coordinates arranged in a
strict hierarchy.

Lambda is the primary coordinate.  It is a single dimensionless number
-- the ratio of inter-particle spacing to merge threshold -- that
governs the temporal regime.  At lambda >> 1, the manifold is folded,
cusp-rich, and drift-fragile, and the taxonomy contains all 10 types.
At lambda ~ 1, the manifold is flat but dynamically anomalous, and the
taxonomy contains 7 types.  At lambda << 1, the manifold is a trivial
hyperplane, and the taxonomy contracts to 6 types that remain frozen
through N = 128.  Lambda explains 99.9% of the temporal structure.

Angle is the secondary coordinate.  It is the launch direction of each
particle, decomposed into radial and tangential components.  Positive
angles produce inward-collapse.  Negative angles produce outward-PBC.
The tangent direction produces stalled or wandering orbits.  Angle
explains 94-97% of the mechanism structure in the bulk sectors.  The
angle partition is topologically protected and persists at every N.

PBC geometry, mediated by radius, is the tertiary coordinate.  It
determines which sub-mechanism variant appears within each angle
sector: direct or bounced for inward collapse, single or glancing for
outward PBC, diagonal for corner proximity.  PBC geometry explains the
3-6% mechanism residual in the bulk and the full 30% residual in the
boundary band.  It is responsible for the 6-member asymptotic taxonomy
(rather than 3), for the boundary band's finite width, and for the
mechanism minorities that prevent any sector from reaching 100% purity.

The 10 sub-mechanism types are the complete output of this hierarchy:

    Angle-protected cores (3):
        IC-direct         (56.2% at N >= 48)
        OPBC-single       (22.6% at N >= 48)
        OL-stalled        ( 6.7% at N >= 48)

    PBC-proximity corrections (3):
        IC-bounced        ( 7.7% at N >= 48)
        CR-diagonal       ( 4.7% at N >= 48)
        OPBC-glancing     ( 2.1% at N >= 48)

    Lambda >> 1 trajectory types (3):
        OPBC-winding      (N = 4 only, < 1% at N = 8)
        DE-tangent        (N = 4 only, gamma = 0, drift = 0)
        DE-frozen         (N = 4 only, gamma = 0, drift = 0)

    Lambda ~ 1 resonance type (1):
        OL-wandering      (21% at N = 8, < 2% at N = 12)

No 11th type can arise because no unoccupied cell exists in the
3 x 3 x 3 generative product.  Every combination of approach direction
(inward, outward, tangential), PBC interaction (interior, edge,
corner), and trajectory regime (instantaneous, marginal, extended)
has been mapped to one of the 10 types or shown to be structurally
identical to one.  The taxonomy is the image of a finite map from a
finite domain, and the image has been fully enumerated.

The closure of the taxonomy closes the ED-Arch formalism.  Laws I
through VIII catalogue the manifold's phenomenology.  Law IX identifies
lambda as the structural origin of the complexity flow.  Law X
identifies angle as the mechanism selector.  Law XI identifies the
boundary band as the transition geometry.  Law XII identifies PBC
geometry as the fine-structure determinant.  Law XIII shows that these
three coordinates generate exactly 10 mechanisms, all observed, none
missing, none redundant.

The ED-Arch manifold is a finite object.  It has three coordinates,
three regimes, three mechanism sectors, two PBC interaction modes, and
exactly 10 sub-mechanism types.  Everything that can happen on a ring
of particles in a periodic box has happened, and has been catalogued.
