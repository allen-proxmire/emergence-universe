Overview

Law VIII established that the five ED-Arch descriptive manifolds are
projections of a single N-parameterized geometric object undergoing a
monotone complexity-reduction flow.  Law IX identifies the structural
origin of that flow.  A single dimensionless quantity -- lambda(N), the
ratio of inter-particle spacing to the merge threshold -- governs the
entire transition from folded richness at small N to planar triviality
at large N.  The complexity flow of Law VIII is the trajectory through
the lambda = 1 crossover: above it, particles have room to explore
before merging, producing mechanism diversity and geometric structure;
below it, collapse is instantaneous and geometry has no time to form.


Formal Statement

Law IX -- The Lambda Invariant and Asymptotic Geometry:

The complexity-reduction flow of Law VIII is governed by a single
dimensionless control parameter, lambda(N) = s(N) / merge_thr, where
s(N) is the mean inter-particle spacing on the initial ring and
merge_thr is the collapse detection threshold.  The unified manifold
occupies three regimes: lambda >> 1 (folded, cusp-rich, drift-fragile),
lambda ~ 1 (resonant plateau), and lambda << 1 (planar, drift-rigid,
instantaneous collapse).  Every geometric, temporal, and taxonomic
property of the manifold is determined by which side of the lambda = 1
crossover the system occupies, and three N values -- one from each
regime -- suffice to predict the full asymptotic structure.


Conceptual Framing

Laws I through VIII describe what the ED-Arch manifold looks like at
each value of N.  Law IX asks why it looks that way.  The answer is
that the manifold's geometry is not controlled by N directly but by the
ratio of two length scales: the spacing between adjacent particles on
the initial ring and the threshold distance at which two particles are
declared merged.  When particles are far apart relative to this
threshold, they execute extended trajectories -- bouncing off PBC walls,
winding through corners, decaying at tangent angles -- and the
mechanism atlas is rich.  When particles are packed so tightly that
their initial spacing is already below the merge threshold, collapse
occurs within a single timestep and no trajectory structure can develop.
The entire phenomenology catalogued in Laws I through VIII -- the
sheets, mirrors, ladders, islands, ridges, resonances, windows, cusps,
folds, commensurability classes, rotational degeneracies, and DECAY
exclusivity -- is the structure that exists in the lambda > 1 regime
and is erased by the lambda < 1 regime.

Lambda is not a new parameter added to the system.  It is a derived
quantity: for a ring of N equally spaced particles with circumradius R
on a box of unit side, the mean inter-particle spacing is
s(N) = 2 * pi * R / N, and the merge threshold is the fixed constant
merge_thr = 23.5 / 400 = 0.05875 in engine units.  As N increases at
fixed circumradius, s(N) decreases as 1/N, so lambda(N) ~ 1/N.  The
lambda = 1 crossover occurs near N = 8, which is precisely where the
manifold undergoes its sharpest geometric transition.


Geometric Interpretation

The lambda invariant explains why the unified manifold is folded at
small N and planar at large N through a geometric argument.

At lambda >> 1 (N = 4), the mean inter-particle spacing is roughly
8.5 times the merge threshold.  Particles launched inward must travel
a distance of order s(N) before encountering a neighbor.  During this
transit, each particle's trajectory is deflected by PBC wall crossings,
corner interactions, and the curvature of the ring itself.  The
mechanism that ultimately produces collapse -- inward contraction,
outward PBC recurrence, corner-mediated collision, or tangential
decay -- depends sensitively on the launch angle, the circumradius,
and the drift.  Small changes in these parameters redirect the
trajectory through a different sequence of PBC interactions, producing
a different mechanism.  This is why the phase boundary at N = 4 has
3 folds, 8 cusps, 5 topological holes, and boundary curvature of
order kappa = 222: the mechanism label is a highly nonlinear function
of the continuous parameters because the trajectory connecting launch
to collapse is long enough to be sensitive to geometry.

At lambda << 1 (N >= 20), the mean inter-particle spacing is already
below the merge threshold at initialization.  Collapse is detected
within a single timestep -- or at most a few timesteps -- regardless
of the launch angle, circumradius, or drift.  No trajectory develops.
No PBC wall is crossed.  No corner is approached.  The mechanism is
determined entirely by the sign of the radial velocity component:
inward launch produces IC-direct, outward launch produces OPBC-single,
and tangential launch at angle = 0 produces OL-stalled.  The phase
boundary between these outcomes depends only on the velocity direction,
which is a smooth function of the angle parameter.  This is why the
manifold is flat: the mechanism label is a nearly linear function of
the continuous parameters because the trajectory connecting launch
to collapse is too short to be deflected by anything.

The transition between these two geometric regimes is not gradual.
Boundary curvature is a delta function concentrated at N = 4.  It is
243.88 at N = 4, exactly zero at N = 8, and remains exactly zero for
all N >= 8 through N = 128.  Torsion into the drift dimension follows
the same pattern: 236.36 at N = 4, exactly zero thereafter.  The
manifold distance D(N) -- a composite measure of curvature, torsion,
boundary-angle residual, and drift rigidity -- drops from 1.46 at
N = 4 to 0.0 at N = 8 in a single step.  This abrupt geometric snap
is the lambda = 1 crossover: the system passes from the regime where
trajectories are long enough to fold the manifold to the regime where
they are not.


The Lambda = 1 Resonance

The N = 8 resonance identified in Laws II and VIII -- a 22% excess of
OL-wandering orbits, a slow temporal plateau (mean chi = 2.74), and a
patchy IC domain with 45 multi-valued slices -- is not an anomalous
exception to the complexity flow.  It is the lambda ~ 1 critical
phenomenon.

At lambda ~ 1, inter-particle spacing is comparable to the merge
threshold.  Particles are close enough that collapse is imminent but
not instantaneous.  A small number of particles -- those launched at
angles that place them in marginally stable orbits relative to their
nearest neighbors -- execute extended trajectories that wind around
the ring before merging.  These are the OL-wandering orbits.  At
lambda >> 1, such marginal orbits are overwhelmed by the diversity of
long-range trajectory types (bounced, corner-mediated, winding).  At
lambda << 1, they cannot exist because collapse occurs before even a
single orbit is completed.  Only at lambda ~ 1 does the marginal-orbit
population emerge as a distinct and measurable fraction of the phase
space.

The N = 8 resonance is unique across the full N = 4 to 128 range.  No
other value of N produces a comparable OL-wandering excess or temporal
plateau.  This uniqueness is explained by the lambda invariant: the
lambda = 1 crossover is traversed exactly once as N increases, and
N = 8 is the value nearest to the crossover point.  At N = 12,
lambda has already fallen well below 1, and the wandering population
drops to 1.4%.  At N = 24, a faint 3.2% echo persists before
vanishing entirely at N = 28.  The resonance is a crossover
phenomenon, not a mechanism.


Regime Structure

The lambda invariant partitions the full N = 4 to 128 parameter space
into three regimes with distinct geometric, temporal, and taxonomic
signatures.

Regime I (lambda >> 1, N = 4): The manifold is folded and
drift-fragile.  Boundary curvature is extreme (mean kappa = 222).
Torsion is large (mean |tau| = 236).  The phase boundary has 3 folds,
8 cusps, and 5 topological holes.  The sub-mechanism taxonomy is at
maximum diversity: 9 distinct types coexist.  Mean collapse time is
chi = 31.6.  A drift perturbation of d = 0.01 changes 25% of cells.
DECAY exists as a 1D filament at angle = 0.

Regime II (lambda ~ 1, N = 8): The manifold is geometrically flat but
dynamically anomalous.  Boundary curvature and torsion are exactly
zero.  The phase boundary is degenerate (onset = +10 degrees at all
radii).  But the temporal landscape carries a slow plateau (mean
chi = 2.74 at positive angles), and the taxonomy includes a 22%
OL-wandering excess not present at any other N.  The IC domain is
patchy.  Drift rigidity is partial (55%).

Regime III (lambda << 1, N >= 20, full asymptotic from N >= 48): The
manifold is a trivial hyperplane.  Curvature, torsion, folds, cusps,
and temporal cliffs are all identically zero.  Chi is at the
measurement floor (0.01) for all N >= 32.  The taxonomy oscillates
within a fixed 5-to-6-member band: {IC-direct, OPBC-single,
IC-bounced, OPBC-glancing, OL-stalled} as the persistent core, with
CR-diagonal and OPBC-winding appearing intermittently as geometric
consequences of the ring-to-box circumradius ratio.  Drift rigidity is
absolute: not a single onset angle changes across drift values of
0.00 to 0.02 at any N >= 8.  IC-direct accounts for approximately
47% of all cells and 85-90% of all IC cells.

The transition between regimes is sharp at the I/II boundary (a
single-step geometric snap) and gradual at the II/III boundary (a
power-law relaxation over approximately 4 doublings of N).


Drift Clause

Drift irrelevance at large N is a direct consequence of instantaneous
collapse.

A drift perturbation adds a small radial velocity component d to each
particle's initial velocity.  When lambda >> 1 and the trajectory from
launch to collapse spans hundreds of timesteps, this perturbation
accumulates over the full trajectory length.  A drift of d = 0.01
displaces each particle by d * chi * dt ~ 0.01 * 22.4 * 0.0025 ~
0.00056 box-lengths over the collapse lifetime at N = 4 -- small in
absolute terms but large relative to the feature scale of the folded
boundary, which has cusps separated by a single radius step.  The
drift destroys the tangent discontinuity because it lifts DECAY orbits
out of the zero-radial-velocity condition that defines them.

When lambda << 1 and collapse occurs within a single timestep, the
same drift perturbation displaces each particle by d * dt ~ 0.01 *
0.0025 ~ 0.000025 box-lengths -- negligible compared to any feature
scale.  The drift has no time to act.  Onset angles, mechanism labels,
sub-mechanism types, and collapse times are all identical at drift = 0
and drift = 0.02 for every N >= 8.  The drift-rigidity index R_drift
locks to exactly 1.000 from N = 8 onward and remains there through
N = 128.  Drift does not gradually become irrelevant; it becomes
irrelevant in a single step as the system crosses lambda = 1.


Temporal Clause

The temporal scaling exponent of the complexity flow -- chi ~ N^(-7/3),
measured as -2.33 with R^2 = 0.978 -- is a consequence of
lambda-controlled geometry interacting with a measurement floor.

The mean collapse time chi measures the typical number of timesteps
from initialization to the first particle merger, normalized to the
decay timescale.  When lambda >> 1, chi is controlled by the trajectory
length: particles must traverse a distance of order s(N) at unit speed,
giving chi ~ s(N) / (speed * dt) ~ R / (N * dt).  Since the
circumradius R depends on the ring configuration and the box-to-ring
ratio, the exact scaling acquires corrections from the PBC geometry
and the angle-dependent velocity projection.  These corrections steepen
the naive 1/N scaling to approximately N^(-7/3).

The measurement floor at chi = 0.01 (collapse within a single
timestep) truncates the power law.  Chi reaches this floor at N = 32
and remains there for all N >= 48.  The measured exponent of -2.33
reflects the true scaling only in the intermediate regime N = 4 to 32
where chi is above the floor.  At larger N, the power law continues
in principle -- particles are even closer and would collapse even
faster -- but the discrete-time simulation cannot resolve sub-timestep
dynamics.  The exponent is a property of the lambda > 0.1 regime;
below that threshold, temporal structure is censored.

The +10-degree onset angle that defines the IC phase boundary at all
N >= 8 is a grid-saturation artifact, not a physical convergence.
The measurement grid samples angles at {-10, -5, -2, -1, 0, +1, +2,
+5, +10} degrees, and +10 degrees is the maximum positive angle.  At
lambda << 1, the true IC boundary -- the angle at which the radial
velocity component is insufficient to produce inward collapse within
the simulation horizon -- moves outward beyond +10 degrees as N
increases.  But because the grid does not sample angles above +10
degrees, the measured boundary saturates at this value.  The apparent
convergence to a fixed onset angle is an artifact of finite angular
resolution.  The physical statement is that at large N, IC occurs at
every measured positive angle, not that the true boundary is at +10
degrees.


Taxonomy Freezing

The sub-mechanism taxonomy does not converge to a single dominant type
as N increases.  Instead it freezes to a narrow 5-to-6-member band
with coefficient of variation 12.6% in taxonomy entropy for N >= 20.
The lambda invariant explains why.

The mechanism label at each parameter cell is determined by the
geometry of the collapse event: which direction the collapsing pair
approaches from (inward vs. outward), whether PBC walls are crossed
(bounced vs. direct), and whether the collapse occurs near a corner
or edge.  At lambda << 1, the dominant pathway is IC-direct (inward
collapse without PBC crossing), which accounts for approximately 47%
of all cells.  But IC-direct cannot approach 100% because the non-IC
region is structurally protected by geometry.  Cells at negative
angles have outward radial velocity and produce OPBC mechanisms
regardless of N.  Cells at angle = 0 have zero radial velocity and
produce OL-stalled.  These geometric niches are topological features
of the velocity-angle parameterization, not dynamical features that
can be erased by reducing lambda.

The intermittent appearance of IC-bounced, CR-diagonal, and
OPBC-winding at specific N values (e.g., IC-bounced at 19% at N = 32,
CR-diagonal at 4% at N = 32) reflects the ring-to-box circumradius
ratio.  At certain N values, the circumradius places particles close
enough to PBC walls that a single-step trajectory crosses a boundary
before merging.  This is a geometric coincidence, not a new mechanism
or a violation of the asymptotic law.  The persistent core of 5
sub-types is the lambda << 1 attractor; the sixth member that appears
intermittently is a finite-size correction that oscillates without
trend.

Taxonomy entropy H_sub converges to a tail mean of 1.12 for N >= 20,
well below the uniform-distribution maximum of H_max ~ 1.61.  The
taxonomy is not maximally diverse (as at N = 4, where H_sub ~ 1.5)
nor maximally concentrated (which would require H_sub ~ 0).  It
occupies an intermediate value set by the geometric partition between
inward and outward velocity regions, which is a property of the
angle parameterization and is independent of N.


Exceptions and Degeneracies

DECAY remains the sole structural singularity.  It is confined
exclusively to N = 4 by Law VII, occupies a 1D filament at angle = 0
and drift = 0, and is destroyed by any nonzero drift.  In the lambda
framework, DECAY is a lambda >> 1 phenomenon: it requires extended
trajectories (chi = 100, the timeout ceiling) that are possible only
when inter-particle spacing greatly exceeds the merge threshold.  At
lambda ~ 1 and below, no particle survives long enough to reach the
DECAY timeout.  The lambda invariant thus provides the structural
explanation for Law VII: DECAY exclusivity is a consequence of the
lambda = 1 crossover eliminating the long-lived trajectories that DECAY
requires.

The faint OL-wandering echo at N = 24 (3.2% versus 22% at N = 8) is
a tail of the lambda ~ 1 resonance, not a new critical phenomenon.
It inflates mean chi slightly above the monotone trend before vanishing
by N = 28.  No other resonance, echo, or anomaly appears at any N in
the range 4 to 128.

The +10-degree grid saturation is not a physical degeneracy.  It is a
measurement limitation that would be resolved by extending the angle
grid to larger positive values.  The underlying physics is that at
lambda << 1, the IC domain expands monotonically in angle as N
increases, eventually exceeding the grid's angular range.

The two-stage structure of the transition -- geometric snap at N = 4
to 8, dynamical relaxation from N = 8 to 48 -- is a consequence of
the different convergence rates of geometric and dynamical observables.
Boundary curvature, torsion, and manifold distance are threshold
quantities that drop to zero as soon as lambda crosses 1.  Collapse
time, taxonomy entropy, and drift sensitivity are continuous quantities
that converge as power laws in lambda.  The distinction between
abrupt and gradual convergence is a property of the observable, not
of the underlying control parameter.


Predictive Power

Three values of N -- one from each lambda regime -- suffice to predict
the full asymptotic structure of the manifold through N = 128.

N = 4 (lambda >> 1) establishes the maximum complexity: the number of
sub-mechanism types, the boundary curvature scale, the temporal range,
and the DECAY filament.  N = 8 (lambda ~ 1) establishes the crossover:
the geometric snap, the OL-wandering resonance, the onset of drift
rigidity, and the flattening of the phase boundary.  N = 12 (lambda < 1)
establishes the power-law trend: the chi exponent, the taxonomy
contraction rate, and the absence of the resonance.  Given these three
calibration points, the manifold's geometry at N = 20, 32, 48, 64, 96,
and 128 is predictable to within measurement precision.

This three-point sufficiency is a consequence of the lambda invariant's
monotonicity.  Because lambda(N) ~ 1/N is strictly decreasing, the
system traverses each regime exactly once.  There are no re-entrant
transitions, no oscillations between regimes, and no secondary
crossovers.  The trajectory through lambda-space is one-dimensional
and monotone, and three points on a monotone curve determine it
completely.


Closing Synthesis

Law IX completes the structural explanation of ED-Arch by identifying
the single quantity that governs all of its phenomenology.

The five descriptive manifolds unified by Law VIII -- mechanism,
boundary, temporal, atlas, and drift -- are projections of a surface
whose geometry is controlled by lambda(N) = spacing / merge_thr.
When lambda is large, the surface is folded, rich, and fragile.  When
lambda is small, the surface is flat, simple, and rigid.  The
complexity-reduction flow that Law VIII describes as a phenomenological
monotone trend is, in structural terms, the system's one-way passage
through the lambda = 1 crossover.

Every feature catalogued in Laws I through VIII has a lambda-level
explanation.  The sheets, mirrors, and ladders of Law I are
lambda >> 1 trajectory structures.  The DECAY islands of Law II are
lambda >> 1 long-lived orbits.  The recurrence windows of Law III are
lambda >> 1 PBC resonances.  The scaling sensitivity of Law IV is the
derivative of lambda with respect to N.  The commensurability classes
of Law V are number-theoretic properties of the ring that matter only
when lambda >> 1.  The N = 4 rotational degeneracy of Law VI is the
point of maximum lambda.  The DECAY exclusivity of Law VII is the
lambda = 1 crossover eliminating long-lived trajectories.  The
complexity flow of Law VIII is the lambda trajectory itself.

Lambda is not an external parameter imposed on the system.  It is an
emergent ratio of two length scales intrinsic to the simulation: the
inter-particle spacing set by the ring geometry, and the merge
threshold set by the collapse criterion.  The entire architecture of
the ED-Arch phase manifold -- its folds, its resonance, its
asymptotic planarity, its drift rigidity, its taxonomic freezing --
is the geometric consequence of a single dimensionless number crossing
unity:

The ED-Arch manifold is the surface traced by lambda(N) as it falls
from richness through resonance to silence.
