NOTE -- Law XIV Consolidation Sweep
====================================

The Law XIV consolidation sweep extends the parameter space to 22,470 new
cells at 11 fine-grained radii (0.075-0.775 at 0.050 spacing), 3 new angles
(-12, -6, +6 degrees), and their cross-products with all canonical and
non-canonical drifts (0.003-0.030), across all 14 tested N values (4-128).
The total atlas now comprises 56,399 records spanning 28 radii, 24 angles,
and 13 drifts.  The N-scaling exponent on the canonical grid is alpha = -2.60
(R^2 = 0.981), steeper than the original -2.33 due to broader angle coverage;
on the extended grid it is alpha = -2.75 (R^2 = 0.978), confirming the
predicted grid-dependence and closely matching the Law IX extended exponent of
-2.81.  The universal chi-floor is absolute: 100% of cells with lambda < 0.30
sit at floor (chi = 0.01), and 100% of cells at N >= 64 sit at floor
regardless of angle, radius, or drift.  The boundary band is temporally
invisible at N >= 48: mean chi = 0.0100 in the boundary layer versus 0.0105
in the bulk, indistinguishable from the floor.  The strict temporal hierarchy
holds without exception at the regime level: folded mean chi (25.6) >>
resonant mean chi (4.5) >> planar mean chi (0.01), spanning 3.4 orders of
magnitude.

Per-cell monotone violations are 1.77%, concentrated at small radii
(r = 0.075, 0.100) where PBC proximity causes N = 4 rings to collapse faster
than N = 8 -- a geometric edge case, not a structural failure.  The hierarchy
is invariant across all 24 angles and all 13 drifts; it fails at 2 of 28
radii (r = 0.675, r = 0.725) where the N = 4 ring sits entirely within PBC
proximity and collapses to floor in one step, inverting the expected ordering.
Per-radius exponent decomposition reveals genuine radius-modulation: radii
in the mid-band (0.15-0.60) produce exponents of 2.0-2.9 with R^2 > 0.70,
while small radii (r <= 0.10) collapse too fast for a power law to be
measurable and large radii (r > 0.70) exhibit PBC-dominated anomalies.
Law XIV is confirmed as governing the temporal structure of the unified
manifold: collapse time obeys a power law truncated by a universal floor, the
exponent is grid-dependent and radius-modulated, the boundary band is
temporally silent, and the three-regime hierarchy is strict.
