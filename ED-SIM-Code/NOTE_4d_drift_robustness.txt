The tangent discontinuity is structurally fragile.  At drift=0 it forms
a near-universal vertical sheet at N=4 (92% of radii show >10pp IC
jump from 0 to +1 degree) and a partial sheet at N=20 (68%), but even
the smallest tested drift (d=0.01) collapses the sheet to WEAK or
DESTROYED at all N, with the jump surviving at only 5/11 radii for N=4
and 1/11 for the others.  By d=0.02 the discontinuity is effectively
gone everywhere.  DECAY, which occupied a 1D filament at (N=4, angle=0)
under zero drift, loses most of its extent at d=0.01 (4/11 radii) and
vanishes entirely at d=0.02.  A surprising finding is that drift
creates new DECAY cells at negative angles: at d=0.01-0.02, DECAY
appears at angle=-1 across multiple radii for N=4, indicating that
inward drift can compensate outward gate components to produce
near-tangent effective velocities.  The N=8 anomaly plateau is
drift-stable: its footprint remains at 39-49% across all tested drift
strengths, never dropping below the PLATEAU threshold.  By contrast,
the large-N anomaly fraction at N=20 shrinks monotonically from 38%
(d=0) to 12% (d=0.10), consistent with drift pushing the system toward
cleaner IC dominance.  Drift sensitivity scales inversely with N at
small perturbations: N=4 is SENSITIVE at d=0.01 (25% cells change),
while N=8,12,20 remain STABLE (5-11%).  All N values become UNSTABLE
by d=0.10.  The phase boundary shifts inward under drift -- at N=20
the IC onset moves from [+1..+10] at d=0 to [-5..+5] at d=0.10,
meaning drift pushes the collapse region into formerly OPBC-dominated
territory.  IC fraction rises monotonically with drift at all N,
reaching 75% at N=20/d=0.10 versus 32% at d=0.  The number of
dominant mechanisms (4-5) and fold/cusp structure are largely
drift-invariant; drift does not create new topological complexity but
rather shifts the existing manifold toward collapse dominance.
