NOTE — 3D Phase Manifold Structure (N x angle x radius)
=========================================================
Date: 2026-03-15
Script: check_3d_manifold.py
Data: n{N}_2d_angle_radius_grid.json (N=4,8,12,20), with provision for
      per-point n{N}_angle_{label}_r{RRR}_sweep.json files at additional N.

The phase boundary separating inward-collapse from PBC/OPBC-dominated
regions forms a connected but topologically non-trivial surface in the
three-dimensional (N, angle, radius) parameter space.  At N=4 and N=20
the boundary is near-vertical in the angle-radius plane — the tangent
discontinuity at 0-to-+1 degree persists across 92% and 68% of radii
respectively, producing a clean vertical sheet.  At N=8 and N=12 the
sheet is perforated: the IC onset angle varies from -10 to +10 degrees
depending on radius, and 37-40% of radii show no IC at any sampled
angle.  The N=8 anomaly is not a localized ridge but a broad plateau,
covering 45% of the angle-radius plane with depressed IC and elevated
other-late fractions.  DECAY occupies an isolated 1D filament in the
full 3D volume: it appears only at (N=4, angle=exactly 0 degrees) and
extends along the radius axis from r~0.07 to r~0.95, confirming its
measure-zero character.  The manifold is heavily folded at small N —
87% of radii at N=4 exhibit cusps where three or more mechanisms
coexist in a single angular slice — but simplifies monotonically with
increasing N, reaching near-binary (IC vs OPBC) partitioning at N=20
with effectively zero folds.  The overall geometry is that of a
crumpled sheet at small N that progressively flattens and stiffens
with increasing particle count.
